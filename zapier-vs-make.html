Zapier vs Make

Zapier and Make (formerly Integromat) are two of the most popular automation platforms for connecting apps and eliminating repetitive work. Both let you build workflows like “when X happens, do Y,” but they differ in ease-of-use, flexibility, and cost structure. This guide helps you choose the right one for your automation needs.

Quick Summary

  • Choose Zapier if you want the simplest setup, fast deployment, and broad app coverage.
  • Choose Make if you want advanced workflow control, complex logic, and cost-efficient scaling.

Zapier Strengths

  • Fast to set up for common workflows
  • Huge library of integrations
  • Simple “trigger → action” approach works for most people
  • Great for lightweight business automations

Make Strengths

  • Powerful visual builder for complex automations
  • More control over data transformations
  • Better for multi-step workflows with branching and error handling
  • Often more cost-efficient when workflows become heavy

Best Use Cases

Zapier is best for:

  • Simple automations across many common tools
  • Quick wins (email alerts, CRM updates, form routing)
  • Non-technical users who want minimal setup

Make is best for:

  • Complex workflows with multiple branches
  • Data-heavy processes (filters, transformations, iterators)
  • Advanced automation that needs monitoring and debugging

Beginner Workflow Examples

  • Lead routing: New Typeform response → add to CRM → notify Slack
  • Meeting follow-ups: New calendar event → create Notion task → send follow-up email draft
  • Content pipeline: New idea note → create draft doc → schedule reminder

Pricing Notes

Both platforms offer free and paid plans. Pricing changes over time, so use each tool’s official pricing page for the latest details. The best approach is to start with one workflow and estimate your monthly task/operation volume.

FAQ

Which is easier?

Zapier is generally easier for beginners. Make is easier once your workflows become complex.

Which is better for complex automations?

Make is typically better for complex, multi-step workflows with branching and data manipulation.

Can I use both?

Yes. Some people use Zapier for quick simple workflows and Make for advanced pipelines.

Final Recommendation

If you want fast results and simplicity, start with Zapier. If you care about flexibility, visual control, and scaling complex workflows, choose Make.
